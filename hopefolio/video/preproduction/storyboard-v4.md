## Selected concept — The spoken faultline

The film opens without a synthetic claim. Hope's actual Config voice says, “I'm less about talk and more about action,” while a single continuity line crosses the woven field and resolves into the new portfolio. The work then proves the statement through the continuity model, the OrgX receipt, and a constraint-based range spectrum. Hope appears once, at the close.

Why it wins:

1. The hook could only belong to Hope: real voice, real public design-community history, and the same action orientation visible in the engineering work.
2. The site and film share one authored system instead of the film becoming a separate ad aesthetic.
3. The visual transformation is causal: principle → thesis → operating model → proof → range → person.
4. The brave choice is restraint. No generic footage, generated people, client-logo tunnel, or fake device chrome.

## Anti-slop check

- Generic corporate footage: absent.
- Meaningless motion: absent; every line, crop, and chromatic split marks inspection or handoff.
- Generic AI gradient: absent.
- Sterile symmetry: absent.
- Product UI invented by AI: absent.
- Could belong to another engineer: no; Config voice, OrgX proof, Hope's portfolio, and Hope's track are inseparable.
